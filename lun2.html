Jeera rice recipe

Little Story

jeera rice recipe | how to make jeera rice | jeera pulao an easy and flavoured rice recipe made with cumin seeds, ghee and basmati rice. it is typically served with choice of dal recipes, preferably dal tadka recipe, but can be served with any gravy based curries.

jeera rice recipe | how to make jeera rice | jeera pulao | jeera rice recipes play a major role in most of the regional indian cuisine. it can range from simple steamed rice to spice-rich biryani recipe which can be served with and without any side dish. one such simple and favourite recipe is jeera rice recipe made with long grain rice and cumin seeds.

in this recipe post, i have showcased 2 ways of making jeera rice recipe, but as i mentioned it can be done in numerous ways. out of 2 options highlighted in this recipe post, my personal favourite is the pressure cooker jeer rice. basically, it is less hassle and quick to prepare. moreover, it requires very little ghee to prepare it.

in other words, it follows the typical steaming approach to cook the rice. having said that, the second option is thrifty and economical. you can use leftover rice to make this and it can be done in few minutes. in addition, this recipe can also be extended by adding finely chopped veggies like onion, beans and green peas to make it an ideal pulao recipe.

Ingredients

½ cup basmati rice

1 tsp oil

½ tsp salt

-------------

1 tsp cumin / jeera

water (to soak & boil)

1 tsp ghee / clarified butter

---------

1 cup water

¼ tsp salt

2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)

Instructions

now in a large heat 1 tsp ghee and splutter 1 tsp cumin seeds.

now add in cooked basmati rice, ¼ tsp salt and mix gently.

finally, add 2 tbsp coriander leaves and enjoy jeera rice with dal.

------------

firstly, in a cooker heat 1 tsp ghee and splutter 1 tsp cumin seeds.

now add ½ cup basmati rice (soaked 20 minutes) and saute for a minute.

add 1 cup of water, ¼ tsp salt and mix well.

------------

cover and pressure cook for 2 whistles.

finally, add 2 tbsp coriander leaves and enjoy jeera rice with dal.
